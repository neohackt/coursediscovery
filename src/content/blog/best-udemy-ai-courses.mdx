## How We Picked These Courses

Not all Udemy courses are created equal. Here is exactly what we looked for:

- **Rating of 4.5 stars or higher** — with a minimum of several thousand reviews. A 4.9-star course with 40 reviews tells you nothing; a 4.5-star course with 50,000 reviews is a genuine signal.
- **Updated in 2025 or 2026** — AI moves fast. A course last updated in 2022 will reference tools and model versions that no longer exist.
- **Clear, distinct use case** — beginner, marketer, developer, freelancer. We avoided courses that try to be everything and end up being nothing.
- **Project-based or practical curriculum** — watching videos does not make you an AI practitioner. Building things does.
- **Reasonable price on sale** — most top Udemy AI courses drop to $10–20 during sales. We flagged anything that consistently overprices itself.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do Udemy AI courses expire?**
No. Once you purchase a course, you have lifetime access. You can revisit it any time, even years later. The one thing that changes is the content — instructors update courses as AI tools evolve, so you may find new sections added over time.

**Are Udemy AI course certificates recognised by employers?**
Udemy certificates confirm course completion and are useful for your LinkedIn profile and resume. They are not accredited credentials. Employers care far more about the projects you can demonstrate than the certificate itself.

**Can I get a refund if a course is not what I expected?**
Yes. Udemy offers a 30-day money-back guarantee on course purchases. If the course does not match what was advertised, you can request a refund within 30 days.

**Which Udemy AI course should a complete beginner start with?**
Start with AI for Everyone — under 3 hours, no coding, no math. Once you understand the landscape, move to The Complete AI Guide for breadth, or a specific track (marketing, development, automation) based on your goal.

**Are there free Udemy AI courses worth taking?**
Yes, but with a caveat. Free Udemy courses tend to be shorter, lighter on content, and less updated than paid courses. They are useful for testing your interest in a topic. For serious learning, the paid catalog — especially at $10–20 during sales — is almost always worth the small investment.

**How long does it take to complete a Udemy AI course?**
Depends on the course. The Complete AI Guide and beginner courses run 8–15 hours. The AI Engineer Course and LLM Engineering courses run 35–50+ hours. At 1–2 hours per day, expect 2–4 weeks for beginner courses and 2–3 months for advanced ones.
